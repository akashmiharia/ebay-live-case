Set the scene · three humans, one live moment

Live commerce runs on three people, and one of them does all the work.

🛍️

The buyer

Job to be done

Win the bid, get the deal, belong to the room, be entertained, do not miss the drop. Trust that the item is real.

📦

The seller

Job to be done

Move real inventory, build a following that comes back, be known. All without the ops swallowing the day.

🎙️

The host

Job to be done

Run the room. Read the crowd, hype the lot, answer, celebrate the regulars. This is the human engine of the sale.

Usually the seller is the host, so one person carries the entire production, live.

First principle

Regular commerce serves demand.
Live commerce manufactures it.

You do not arrive wanting the glossy green dress. The room makes you want it: the auction thrill, the "you got it for that price," a stranger measuring the dress so you bid. Belonging forms the intent, live. Amazon and Walmart structurally cannot do that.

The problem, stated

eBay promises trusted community commerce.
It ships a glitchy, tool-starved broadcast, to almost no one.

Live is still a gated private beta. Most sellers cannot even get in, and the few who do run it by hand on a stack that breaks under them. The gap between the promise and the delivery is the entire space this role sits in.

Validate against the market · access vs exclusivity

Whatnot democratized live, almost to a fault. eBay gentrified it.

Who can sellAnyone with an ID and a bank accountProven, established sellers only
Time to go live~15 min sign-up, days to approveApplication waitlist, months or never
The resultCutthroat, thousands live at once, race to the bottomProtected, quiet, and empty
Trust modelBan the scammer after the factVet the person before the fact

Whatnot is the Wild West: easy, alive, and lawless. eBay Live is a gated country club: safe, quiet, and you are probably not on the list. The question is why eBay can't just open the gate.

The insight · capability, not permission

eBay isn't gating Live to be exclusive.
It's gating because it can't police live at velocity.

On async eBaya scammer's 50 fake Rolexes get caught by algorithms before the money moves. Damage is slow and contained.
On livethe same person sells 50 fakes in ten minutes to 500 people and vanishes. No bot polices real-time audio and video.
So eBaypolices the person instead of the content, and only hands the live weapon to sellers with too much to lose. Four years, and the gate never opens.

The trust moat and the beta gate are the same fact. An agent that verifies trust at live velocity is the only thing that lets Live graduate out of beta.

Why eBay wins · the only uncopyable thing

It's not luxury. Whatnot has watches too.
It's the data and trust under every seller.

Thirty years of catalog, sold-comps, purchase history, and a real authentication operation, sitting behind millions of established sellers. Not a feature Whatnot can ship past. It is exactly what an agent needs to stand up a trusted show for you, and to keep it safe in real time.

The design principle that decides everything

Humans do the human part. The agent does the rest.

Human, never touched

The chat, the banter, crowning Jeremy, reading the room. This connection is the whole reason people come to live instead of scrolling AI-generated feeds. Automate it and you kill it.

Agent, everything else

Standing up the show, pricing from comps, verifying trust, moderating, shipping, disputes, logistics. The repetitive, high-stakes, low-connection work that drowns the seller today.

Differentiation · below / table stakes / differentiate

Where we lose on purpose, tie, and win.

Below · trade it away

  • Out-clowning Whatnot's youth culture (their moat, fake on eBay)
  • Breadth of cheap, impulse categories
  • Being the lowest-fee option

Table stakes · must match

  • Go live from a phone, fast payout, discovery
  • Auction mechanics that don't glitch
  • Kudos and loyalty, AI listing (eBay already ships these)

Differentiate · where we win

  • Trust at velocity: real-time moderation and fraud checks that let eBay open the gate
  • Authenticity Guarantee brought to live (verified after the sale) plus computer-vision confidence signals in the moment
  • One-tap trusted supply from data Whatnot doesn't have
The hard part · picking, with the trade-off named

Fix supply first. The buyer follows the show.

TodayAn ops co-pilot for existing beta hosts. Real, but a small gated population. Low ceiling.
The betTrust at velocity plus one-tap supply. This is what graduates Live out of beta, and it is the whole GMV unlock.
The trade-offSupply-first risks thin quality per show. We accept it: an empty marketplace has no quality problem yet. Liquidity precedes polish.
The loopFix the seller, the shows get good, and the culture buyer eBay doesn't have yet finally shows up. Supply earns the audience.

How we measure success

Supply first. Then GMV. Trust as the guardrail.

Activation
Dormant sellers who go live for the first time, and time from tap to live show.
Liquidity
Active hosts, shows per week, hours live. Is the marketplace filling up?
North star
Live GMV, from newly activated supply. Everything ladders here.
Guardrail
Dispute rate and authentication pass-through. We buy liquidity, we never sell trust to get it.
